The Furniture Dilemma in Kenya

Modular furniture is not the norm in Nairobi. People tend to buy from stores or request something custom from their fundi or craftsman. Each approach has its advantages. Furniture from stores guarantee quality, while fundi-made furniture allows for a high degree of customization. On the flip side, store-bought furniture often doesn't fit a buyer's exact needs, and while there are many talented fundis in the city, it can be difficult to find one whose quality is up to your standards. So how do you find furniture that is both of exceptional quality and customizable? That's where modular design comes in. It is intricately engineered to ensure quality and reliability, while being flexible enough to arrange into a custom piece.

The Evolution of Modular Furniture

Modular furniture has a long history, but is relatively new to Kenya. The more common concept in the furniture world is that of flat-pack, in which industrially-produced furniture is sold as a kit of parts. The customer receives their furniture as a box of parts, along with some simple tools and instructions, and they assemble the furniture themselves.

Such offerings, most famously from IKEA, are designed to save as much space in transportation as possible. This makes sense from an industrial-scale, commercial sense, where goods are shipped globally, and every inch of trucks and shipping containers is best left unused.

For a Kenyan furniture brand, however, we have found that focusing on flat pack might not be the best solution. This is because flat pack solutions are more complex for customers to assemble, which can often cause frustration.

Our modular system provides the best of both worlds. It is demountable into easily transportable units, but setup is as easy as stacking units together, with no tools required.

Why Modular Furniture Makes Sense for Kenyan Homes

Adapting to Urban Living Spaces

Nairobi's hosuing landscape is evolving rapidly. Apartments are becoming smaller and more efficient, while homeowners increasingly value flexibility. Traditional bulky furniture that dominated Kenyan homes for generations is giving way to more adaptable solutions that can transform as needs change.

Modular shelving systems are perfect for these modern living conditions because they:

  • Can fit into corners or span entire walls
  • Allow for expansion as collections grow
  • Can be reconfigured when moving houses
  • Provide storage solutions without overwhelming a space
